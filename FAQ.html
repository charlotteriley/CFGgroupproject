Pigeon FAQs

  • What do you feed pigeons?

    Pigeons will most eat seed, grains and legumes. They will sometimes eat an insect, small snail or worm in order to obtain calcium or extra protein when breeding. Unlike most birds they suck up water, gently place their beaks in water without covering the nostrils and they will drink.

  • How do you know if your pigeon is sick?

    Some signs of a sick or injured pigeon include; sitting apart from the flock, difficulty keeping eyes open, walking in circles, throwing seeds into the air, totters backwards, fits, using wings to walk or is unable to fly.

  • Are pigeons social animals?

    Pigeons are social animals, they tend to be seen in flocks of 20-30 birds. They will also bond with humans easily, especially one that feeds them, they will become a faithful companion to its human.

  • How long will a pet pigeon live for?

    The average lifespan of a pet pigeons tends to be between eight to twelve years.

  • Should a pet pigeon be kept in a cage?

    Your pet pigeons needs an appropriate cage as their home base and safe place if they are living in the house. They also need safe, daily out-of-cage time in the house to exercise and socialize. An indoor pigeon shouldn't only be taken outside in a predator proof cage as they are easy targets.
